# Annsetu Admin Operations System

## Overview
The Admin Operations Panel is the central control plane for the farmer -> hub -> customer supply chain. It manages procurement, inventory, orders, delivery, pricing, payments, and support with real-time visibility.

## Roles and access
- Super Admin: full system control, pricing, finance, and policy overrides.
- Hub Manager: hub operations, inventory, orders, and staffing.
- Inventory Manager: batches, expiry, quality grading, and waste.
- Delivery Manager: routes, riders, ETA, and SLA recovery.
- Support Executive: tickets, refunds, and customer history.

## Navigation structure
- Ops Center: realtime KPIs, alerts, and live activity.
- Farmers: onboarding, KYC, performance, and payout readiness.
- Procurement: demand-based intake, schedules, and grading.
- Inventory: batch tracking, FIFO, expiry, and damage control.
- Hub Ops: staffing, packaging workflow, and zone utilization.
- Orders: allocation, packing, exceptions, and refunds.
- Delivery: routes, heatmaps, rider assignment, and ETA.
- Pricing: dynamic pricing, discounts, and surge logic.
- Payments: settlements, expenses, and profit tracking.
- Support: ticket desk, quality issues, and refunds.
- Analytics: forecasting, retention, hub benchmarks.
- System: roles, audit logs, and alert rules.

## Core workflows
### Procurement workflow
1. Demand forecast produces SKU targets by hub and zone.
2. Farmers submit stock offers with expected quantities and quality.
3. Admin approves, rejects, or adjusts quantities and price.
4. Pickup schedule created by village and route.
5. Batches arrive at hub, quality graded, and posted to inventory.

### Inventory workflow
1. Batch creation on arrival with product, farmer, cost, and expiry.
2. FIFO allocation to orders by earliest expiry.
3. Near-expiry triggers automatic discount or transfer.
4. Damage and wastage logged with root-cause tags.

### Order workflow
1. Orders placed and inventory allocated.
2. Pack lists generated by zone and time window.
3. Exceptions raised for missing or damaged items.
4. Refund or replacement triggered based on policy.

### Delivery workflow
1. Orders grouped into delivery batches by zone.
2. Routes optimized with ETA and rider capacity.
3. SLA alerts for delays and failed deliveries.
4. Feedback loop into demand and rider planning.

### Pricing workflow
1. Base price set from procurement cost and target margin.
2. Demand and inventory coverage adjust price dynamically.
3. Near-expiry markdowns auto-triggered.
4. Campaigns override pricing with time windows.

### Financial workflow
1. Customer payments captured and reconciled.
2. Farmer settlements executed weekly.
3. Delivery and staffing payouts processed by hub.
4. Profit and loss tracked by hub, category, and day.

## Data model and relationships
- Hub (1) -> InventoryBatch (many)
- Farmer (1) -> ProcurementOffer (many)
- ProcurementOffer (1) -> InventoryBatch (many)
- Product (1) -> InventoryBatch (many)
- InventoryBatch (many) -> OrderItem (many)
- Order (1) -> OrderItem (many)
- Order (1) -> DeliveryRun (1)
- DeliveryRun (1) -> DeliveryStop (many)
- Customer (1) -> Order (many)
- Ticket (many) -> Order (1)
- Payout (many) -> Farmer (1)
- PricingRule (many) -> Product (many)

## Backend logic modules
- Auth and RBAC: JWT, role-based permissions, session audit.
- Procurement service: demand targets, approvals, and scheduling.
- Inventory service: batch creation, FIFO allocation, expiry rules.
- Order service: allocation, packing, and exception handling.
- Delivery service: batching, routing, ETA tracking.
- Pricing engine: rule evaluation and campaign overrides.
- Finance service: settlements, expenses, profit calculation.
- Support service: tickets, refunds, and replacements.
- Notification service: alert rules and escalation.
- Reporting service: daily, weekly, and ad-hoc exports.

## KPIs and dashboards
- Orders, delivery SLA, and fulfillment rate.
- Inventory cover, near-expiry risk, and wastage loss.
- Procurement fill rate and farmer reliability.
- Revenue, margin, and operational cost per hub.
- Ticket resolution time and refund rate.

## Multi-hub scaling
- Hub-specific inventory pools with cross-hub transfers.
- City-level dashboards with drill down to hub and zone.
- Shared pricing rules with local overrides.
- Regional procurement planning by crop and season.

## Operational guardrails
- SLA thresholds for delivery and pack completion.
- Quality grading rules by SKU.
- Wastage thresholds with root-cause tags.
- Audit trail for price changes and settlement approvals.

- roles: id, name, permissions_json.
- hubs: id, name, city, capacity, status.
- zones: id, hub_id, name, coverage_km.
- farmers: id, name, kyc_status, rating, hub_id.
- products: id, name, category, unit, shelf_life_hours.
- procurement_offers: id, farmer_id, hub_id, status, offer_date.
- procurement_lines: id, offer_id, product_id, qty, price, grade.
- inventory_batches: id, product_id, farmer_id, hub_id, qty, cost, grade, arrival_at, expiry_at, status.
- inventory_movements: id, batch_id, movement_type, qty, reason, created_at.
- orders: id, customer_id, hub_id, status, total, placed_at.
- order_items: id, order_id, product_id, batch_id, qty, price.
- delivery_runs: id, hub_id, status, driver_id, route_km, eta_minutes.
- delivery_stops: id, run_id, order_id, seq, status.
- pricing_rules: id, name, scope, rule_json, active.
- payments: id, order_id, amount, status, method, paid_at.
- payouts: id, farmer_id, amount, status, scheduled_at, settled_at.
- expenses: id, hub_id, category, amount, incurred_at.
- tickets: id, order_id, category, status, assigned_to, created_at.
- audit_logs: id, actor_id, action, entity, entity_id, created_at.
- notifications: id, type, severity, payload_json, status, created_at.
